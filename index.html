THRIVE: A Parent\'92s Resource for Parenting

\ \ \
\

The Clearinghouse for Military Family Readiness at Penn State in partnership with the Department of Defense\'92s Office of Military Community and Family Policy is implementing the THRIVE Initiative to empower parents as they nurture their children from the prenatal period until their children reach 18 years of age. The Initiative includes a series of face-to-face and online parenting programs and a range of online resources and interactive learning modules. Each THRIVE program guides parents in learning and using parenting and health promotion skills that are based on science. By supporting parents as they learn and practice effective strategies for raising healthy children, THRIVE fosters Resourceful Parents, Resilient Children, and Ready Families!

\

The initiative consists of four age-specific skill-building segments: Take Root, Sprout, Grow, and Branch Out.

\

For parents of newborn to 3 years

\


\ Having a baby is exciting, but parenting an infant can be confusing and stressful. The Take Root! parenting program, which is currently being developed in three parts (i.e., Take Root! Newborn-6 months, Take Root! 7-12 months, and Take Root! 1 to 3 years), will serve to support parents from pregnancy through the first three years of their child\'92s life. Each program emphasizes positive parenting, stress management, and health promotion skills that can be used to help parents raise a happy and healthy baby. Parents are able to participate in all programs online, which means they can access the program\'92s information whenever it is most convenient for them.

For parents of children 3 to 5 years

\


\ Calling all parents of preschoolers! Sprout! is the THRIVE program area that specifically targets the unique context of parenting children between the ages of three and five. As parents know, this developmental period comes with many parenting joys and challenges. Sprout! is designed to support parents as they foster their preschool child\'92s development and resiliency. Sprout! is presently under development; once available, a series of face-to-face sessions will be offered on days and during times of the week that best fit into families\'92 busy schedules.

\

For parents of children 5 to 10 years

\


\ Raising a young child is rewarding, but it is also challenging. As children grow and become more independent, they are exposed to external influences, like peers and media entertainment. The good news is that parents can remain a primary guiding force in helping their child mature into a responsible, caring, healthy, and competent adult. The Grow! parenting program is designed to help parents of 5- to 8-year-olds learn positive parenting techniques, stress management strategies, and health promotion skills that can be used to help children be happy and healthy. Currently, the program is offered in an in-person, group-based format with five sessions.

\

For parents of children 10 to 14 years

\

\

Do you have a middle school or high school-aged child at home? If so, then the Branch Out! parenting program may be for you! During adolescence, kids need their parents more than ever. With so many changes and transitions happening during adolescence, this phase of life is a time of both excitement and anxiety\uc0\u8213 for both adolescents and their parents. Branch Out! is designed to support mothers and fathers in their parenting role, as they in turn nurture their child as they transition from child- to adulthood. As with the other programs encompassed within the THRIVE initiative, the program will focus on positive parenting practices, stress management strategies, and health promotion skills. Branch Out! is scheduled for development in 2017. Further information will be made available on this website soon, as the program curriculum takes shape.
